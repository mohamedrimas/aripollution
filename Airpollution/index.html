Development of a predictive IOT-based system for real-time air quality monitoring and route optimization

A Cutting-Edge IoT System for Real-Time Air Quality Monitoring and Travel Route Optimization

Read More


Why Choose the pollution mobile care app ?

Air pollution, especially in urban areas, is a critical issue, with over 60% of pollution in Colombo, Sri Lanka, coming from vehicle emissions. Pollutants like CO2, NO2, CH4, and PM2.5 cause serious health problems, including asthma and heart disease. Real-time monitoring systems are essential to manage air quality and protect public health.

Recent IoT advancements have enabled systems to collect real-time pollution data, but challenges include data transmission delays, inaccurate predictions, high infrastructure costs, and lack of user-focused solutions. This research proposes an IoT-based system integrating air quality sensors with machine learning models (Random Forest, Prophet) for accurate predictions and real-time travel recommendations. This cost-effective solution improves urban air quality management and public health outcomes.

Real-Time IoT Monitoring

Sri Lanka faces a significant air pollution crisis, primarily driven by vehicular emissions, impacting public health with rising mortality rates. Key pollutants include Particulate Matter (PM), Carbon Dioxide (CO₂), and Methane (CH₄), posing severe health risks. Our IoT-based Air Pollution Monitoring System aims to monitor real-time air quality levels, utilizing Arduino UNO with MQ7 and MQ4 sensors to detect CH₄ and CO gases, alongside an ESP8266 Wi-Fi module to transmit data every minute to a central database. When pollution exceeds safe levels, the system notifies users via mobile application, providing an accessible, portable, and cost-effective solution for air quality management

Pollution Levels Prediction

Utilization of machine learning techniques to predict air pollution levels relies on historical data and environmental factors. This process enables the early identification of significant pollutants, such as CO2, PM2.5, and PM10, through rigorous analysis and feature engineering. Data cleaning and preparation ensure the integrity of the dataset, which is divided into an 80/20 split for training and testing to enhance model generalizability.

Various machine learning models, including Random Forest, XGBoost, and Prophet, are employed to deliver accurate air quality predictions. This comprehensive approach supports effective monitoring and management of air quality levels, providing valuable insights for environmental health and policy-making.

Real-Time Pollution Map and Risk Exposure Analysis

Provide a comprehensive and user-centered solution for monitoring air quality in real time. It includes gathering pollutant data from IoT devices, such as CO2, NO2, and CH4 levels, across various geographic locations. This data is then transmitted through an MQTT Broker and processed to generate real-time, interactive heatmap that visually represents pollution intensity in specific areas. The system continuously analyzes pollution data, checking if pollutant levels exceed safe thresholds. When this happens, it identifies users within affected areas and sends them personalized notifications push to their health conditions via Firebase for Android devices and APNs for iOS devices. The key features that it provides health-focused, user-specific alerts, making it especially beneficial for vulnerable populations, such as those with respiratory conditions, by allowing them to take immediate action based on their locations. Including a location service that periodically updates pollution levels, ensuring that users always have access to the most current data

Alternative Route Suggestion

In response to Colombo urban air pollution crisis, we developed a real-time route optimization system to minimize exposure to pollutants. Utilizing a shortest-route algorithm paired with dynamic mapping, this mobile application processes real-time pollution data and calculates the least polluted paths. The backend server continuously updates route options based on pollution and distance. By offering users optimal travel routes, this system supports healthier commuting choices, reducing pollution exposure and contributing to improved public health.

About the Project

Sri Lanka faces severe air pollution challenges, largely due to vehicular emissions, which threaten public health. To address this, we developed a real-time air quality monitoring system that visualizes pollution via a heatmap. The system inte grates gas sensors monitoring methane (CH), carbon monoxide (CO), carbon dioxide (CO), sulfur dioxide (SO), nitrogen dioxide (NO), and particulate matter (PM.). These sensors are connected to an ESP32 board and Wi-Fi module, strategically deployed around smart buildings. Data is transmitted to a centralized hub using IoT technology, enabling real-time visualization and storage. The processed data is presented through a mobile app developed with Flutter, employing a color-coded scheme to represent pollution levels. This allows users to identify high risk areas and choose less polluted travel routes. In testing, we focused on predicting PM. concentrations in Colombo using machine learning algorithms like Random Forest, XGBoost, and Prophet. The Prophet model was most effective for CO prediction, while Random Forest excelled in predicting PM. concentrations, demonstrating the potential of machine learning in improving air quality forecasts. The system also features a MIMI Air device, which collects, processes, and transmits air quality data every 30 seconds, triggering real-time alerts when pollutant levels exceed WHO thresholds. This provides a cost effective, portable, and reliable solution for managing air quality and safeguarding public health.

Literature Survey

While existing systems have made significant progress, challenges remain in terms of real-time data accuracy, user-focused solutions, and improving accessibility for vulnerable groups like school-children. These gaps highlight the need for more integrated approaches that combine monitoring, prediction, and user-centric tools to effectively manage air quality and reduce exposure in urban areas.

Research Gap

Many organizations in Sri Lanka, such as Central Environmental Authority (CEA), National Building Research Organization (NBRO), Department of Motor Traffic (DMT), etc. monitor the ambient air quality in Sri Lanka. The Central Environmental Authority has deployed two fixed automated stations deployed in Battaramulla and Kandy, to monitor the ambient air quality continuously. The mobile station is currently in progress state. Apart from this method, the most used monitoring method is the passive monitoring network which was developed by the National Building Research Organization and also they are currently working on a sensor deployment to monitor ambient air pollution. Therefore, the current existing air quality monitoring system in Sri Lanka is still in a research state and need an improvement to increase usability.

Research Problem

How can air quality monitoring and prediction be effectively integrated into an IoT-based system that forecasts environmental risks and minimizes public exposure to pollutants, particularly in urban areas like Colombo, Sri Lanka? This research seeks to:

  • Define air pollution within the context of IoT technology for real-time data collection.
  • Develop a user-friendly pollution map to make data easily understandable.
  • Encourage low-pollution travel options based on predictive models.
  • Enhance public transparency and awareness about pollution levels.
  • Address the impact of air pollution on vulnerable groups, especially schoolchildren.

Research Objectives

Main Objective

Minimize the adverse effects of air pollution for the general public population while travelling.

Sub Objectives

Develop sensor-based devices with a focus on accuracy and value creation on Air pollution.

Implement future pollution level prediction and forecasting mechanism

Create a user-friendly real-time pollution heatmap accessible to all

Develop real-time route generation based on pollution levels





Methodology

1.Literature Review:Conduct an extensive review of IoT-based air quality monitoring systems, machine learning models for pollution prediction, and route optimization technologies

2. Requirement Gathering:Engage with environmental experts, urban planners, and public health officials to gather system requirements and preferences for air quality monitoring and route suggestion.

3.Application Design:Develop system architecture, wireframes, and prototypes, integrating air quality sensors, real-time data transmission, and machine learning algorithms

4.Development: Utilize agile methodologies to develop the system in phases, incorporating feedback from stakeholders on sensor integration, heatmap visualization, and route optimization

5. Testing:Conduct rigorous testing, including real-time sensor accuracy, system reliability, usability for travel route suggestions, and prediction accuracy.

6. Deployment: Implement the system in an urban area like Colombo, with sensors placed in strategic locations, and launch the web and mobile platforms for public use.

7. Evaluation and Improvement: Gather user feedback and evaluate the system's effectiveness in reducing pollution exposure and improving travel route decisions, continuously refining the system based on feedback.
